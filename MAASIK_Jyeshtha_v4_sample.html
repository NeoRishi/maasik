02 · WHAT'S HAPPENING

The fire turns inward

Here is the surprise: in summer, your digestion tends to get weaker, not stronger.

→
Peak Summer
heat moves to the skin

In peak summer, your body pushes heat outward to cool you down. The gut runs quieter. Hungry people still feel heavy after heavy meals.

Word Origin
Agni
Literally fire. In Ayurveda, the metabolic fire that digests food. The same Sanskrit root gives English igneous, rocks formed from fire.

For an Anchored Builder, two patterns tend to compound. Your body already runs warm, so the season's heat lands on a warm system. Your build holds weight when desk work replaces movement.

The plan splits in two.

Front 1
Cool the internal heat
Cooling foods. Smaller portions. No chillies, no fermented food. Coconut water and cumin-fennel tea instead of coffee and cold drinks.
Front 2
Lighten the structural load
Lighter dinners. Morning movement before the heat. Fewer fried and heavy foods. No daytime nap over 20 minutes.

05 · FIVE ANCHORS

Five rules that carry the rest

If you hold only these five, the rest of the blueprint follows.

  1. Lunch is your biggest meal

    12:30 to 1:30. Eat enough that you do not need a 4 PM rescue.

  2. Dinner is light, before 7:30

    Khichadi, soup, or one roti and a vegetable. Visibly smaller than lunch.

  3. Walk before 8 AM

    Twenty minutes. After 10 AM the heat tends to work against you.

  4. Coconut water replaces snacks

    Same 4:30 PM break. Less load on the body.

  5. Sleep by 11 PM

    Coconut oil on the soles tends to help settle the heat before bed.

Three things to actively avoid
  • Pani puri, pizza, burger more than twice this month.
  • Outdoor exercise between 10 AM and 5 PM.
  • Coffee after 2 PM.

07 · YOUR COMMITMENT

The one thing

Hrishikesh, here is the thread.

Your archetype runs on depth and steadiness. The same density that gives your work weight (the good kind) tends to hold weight (the bodily kind) when desk work replaces movement. Greeshma's heat layers warming on top.

The pizza, the burger, the pani puri are not failures. They are heating and heavy at once, the one combination this body and this season can least afford.

"Lunch big. Dinner small. Walk before 8." Repeat for thirty days

Jyeshtha is not asking you to give things up. It is asking you to slow, cool, and eat with the season. That is not a restriction. It is a return.

"Eat wholesomely, eat moderately, eat in tune with the season."
